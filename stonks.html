As my first project aside from my courses' homework, I decided to develop a finance web-app.
Being active on the stock market myself and using various fintech websites and apps, it was appealing to try to design a (basic) service for checking stocks myself. As an homage to a well-known sub-reddit I named it stonks (= stocks).

Idea

Stock Screener

This web-app aims to make it as easy as possible to search for stocks and screen them based on performance indicators, comparable stats in their peer-group and additional information like current news.

A constraint I found early in development was that I needed to limit the app to NYSE-listed US-stocks - I would have preferred to a t least include european stocks as well, but the accessible APIs I found are focused on the US-market.

Goal

Responsiveness

Optimized usability on mobile as well as desktop was important for me. Working with financial data can easily be overwhelming. Especially on small screens - hence I strived to create an equal user experience for users on all device-sizes.

Bootstrap helped a lot by offering responsive breakpoints, that i utilized to rearrange data-boxes in rows/columns, change font-sizes and leaving out less important elements like a company's logo when on mobile.

On top of optimizing the device-reponsiveness I wanted to implement a simple darkmode to enhance the user experience for different situations and preferences. For this, I created a JavaScript-function that toggles between dark- & lightmode-CSS-attributes.

Challenges

Finding my current limitations

Beeing still very early on the way to become a developer, I found various constraints and problems, that I hope to solve later on.

Speed:

If you tried the app, you'll have noticed: the collection of the data for a selected stock and processing it in the backend takes quite long. I'm not sure if it is simply bad coding on my side or if the limitation lies in the API-responses. But I'm going to find out.

Bootstrap nuances:

I really enjoyed building the frontend with Bootstrap - although by striving to create a unqiue look & feel for stonks, I had to alter more and more elements. My solution was creating an own stylesheet and overwrite the Bootstrap-styles. I know that's not the elegant and recommended way - and I want to deepen my understanding of Bootstrap and CSS by utilizing Sass-files in the future.
